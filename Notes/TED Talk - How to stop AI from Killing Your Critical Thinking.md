Guess who gave this talk?
**Advait Sarkar**. The author of [[When Copilot Becomes Autopilot Generative AI's Critical Risk to Knowledge Work and a Critical Solution (2412.15030v1)]]

Video Link: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=3lPnN8omdPA

Their research prototype is called **Tools for Thought**.
Efficiency is not their aim, better thinking is.

The user engages more with the material. GenAI is used to create provocations and strengthen the argument rather than do the writing for them. Users can also quickly change the tone of a paragraph, etc. So genAI helps speed up the process but it it does not automate thinking for the user. The is **no chatbot** where the user can go and ask AI to do the work for them. 

With good design and intention, these principles can be applied to different areas of AI adoption.

Closing question: "What would you rather have? A tool that thinks for you, or a tool that makes you think."
